Virat Kohli, an Indian cricketer, holds the record for being the fastest player to score 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 runs in One Day International (ODI) cricket. He achieved each milestone in fewer innings than any other player in the history of the game. Kohli's incredible consistency and remarkable batting skills have earned him numerous accolades and established him as one of the greatest cricketers of his generation.
In 2018, Virat Kohli became the first Indian cricketer to score three centuries in a single series on Australian soil. During the four-match Test series against Australia, Kohli showcased his exceptional batting prowess by scoring centuries in the first, second, and third Test matches held in Adelaide, Perth, and Melbourne, respectively. His brilliant performances played a significant role in India's historic Test series victory in Australia, marking a remarkable milestone in his career.
